Women's Health - Hormones, Menstrual Health, and Menopause

Women's health encompasses a wide range of physical and mental health concerns that are unique to or more common in women. Understanding these conditions promotes better health outcomes.

Menstrual Health:
A normal menstrual cycle lasts between 21 to 35 days and periods typically last 2 to 7 days. Common menstrual problems include dysmenorrhea (painful periods), menorrhagia (heavy periods), amenorrhea (absent periods), and premenstrual syndrome (PMS). PMS symptoms include bloating, breast tenderness, mood swings, irritability, fatigue, and food cravings occurring in the days before menstruation. Treatment for PMS includes regular exercise, dietary changes, stress management, and in some cases hormonal contraceptives or antidepressants.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS):
PCOS is a hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age. Symptoms include irregular periods, excess androgen (causing facial hair and acne), polycystic ovaries visible on ultrasound, weight gain, difficulty losing weight, and fertility problems. Management includes lifestyle changes (diet and exercise for weight management), hormonal birth control to regulate periods, metformin for insulin resistance, and fertility treatments for those trying to conceive.

Endometriosis:
Endometriosis is a condition where tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus causing severe pain. Symptoms include extremely painful periods, chronic pelvic pain, pain during intercourse, pain with bowel movements or urination, excessive bleeding, and infertility. Treatment options include pain medications, hormonal therapies, and surgery to remove endometrial tissue.

Menopause:
Menopause is a natural biological process marking the end of menstrual cycles, diagnosed after 12 consecutive months without a period, typically occurring in women in their late 40s to early 50s. Symptoms include hot flashes, night sweats, vaginal dryness, sleep problems, mood changes, weight gain, slowed metabolism, and thinning hair. Perimenopause is the transition period before menopause lasting several years. Treatment options include hormone replacement therapy (HRT), non-hormonal medications, lifestyle modifications, and complementary therapies. HRT is effective but discuss risks and benefits with your doctor.

Breast Health:
Women should perform monthly breast self-exams and have regular clinical breast exams. Mammograms are recommended annually or biennially starting at age 40 to 50 depending on individual risk factors. See a doctor promptly for any new breast lump, nipple discharge, skin dimpling, or breast pain.

Cervical Health:
Regular Pap smears detect cervical cancer early. Guidelines recommend Pap smears every 3 years from age 21 to 65, or every 5 years combined with HPV testing from age 30 to 65. The HPV vaccine protects against the strains of HPV most commonly associated with cervical cancer and is recommended for girls and boys aged 11 to 12.

Bone Health and Osteoporosis:
Women are at higher risk of osteoporosis especially after menopause due to decreased estrogen. Adequate calcium (1000 to 1200 mg daily) and vitamin D (600 to 800 IU daily) intake, regular weight-bearing exercise, avoiding smoking and excess alcohol, and bone density testing (DEXA scan) after age 65 are important preventive measures.

When to See a Gynecologist:
Annual well-woman visits are recommended for all adult women. Seek prompt care for abnormal vaginal bleeding or discharge, pelvic pain, pain during intercourse, urinary symptoms, or any breast changes.
