Common Skin Conditions - Eczema, Psoriasis, and Acne

The skin is the body's largest organ and is affected by many conditions. Understanding common skin conditions helps with early recognition and proper treatment.

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis):
Eczema is a chronic inflammatory skin condition causing dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. It commonly begins in childhood but can occur at any age. Symptoms include intense itching, dry cracked skin, red to brownish-gray patches, small raised bumps that may weep fluid, and thickened or scaly skin. Eczema flares are triggered by dry skin, sweating, stress, soaps and detergents, dust mites, animal dander, and certain foods. Treatment includes regular moisturizing with fragrance-free creams, topical corticosteroids during flares, antihistamines for itching, topical calcineurin inhibitors, and biologic medications for severe cases. Avoid hot showers and harsh soaps.

Psoriasis:
Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition causing rapid buildup of skin cells resulting in scaling on the skin surface. Symptoms include red patches covered with thick silvery scales, dry cracked skin that may bleed, itching burning or soreness, thickened pitted or ridged nails, and swollen stiff joints (psoriatic arthritis). Triggers include stress, infections (especially streptococcal throat infection), injuries to skin, smoking, alcohol, and certain medications. Treatment options include topical creams (corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues, retinoids), phototherapy (light therapy), systemic medications (methotrexate, cyclosporine), and biologic drugs targeting specific immune pathways.

Acne Vulgaris:
Acne is the most common skin condition affecting up to 85% of people at some point. It occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. Types include whiteheads, blackheads, papules, pustules, nodules, and cystic acne. Causes include excess oil production, bacteria (Cutibacterium acnes), hormonal changes, certain medications, and diet. Treatment depends on severity. Mild acne responds to over-the-counter products containing benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, or adapalene. Moderate to severe acne may require prescription topical retinoids, antibiotics, combined oral contraceptives for women, isotretinoin for severe cases. Never pop or squeeze pimples as this worsens inflammation and causes scarring.

Hives (Urticaria):
Hives are raised itchy welts on the skin caused by allergic reactions, infections, stress, or medications. They typically resolve within 24 hours. Antihistamines are the first line treatment. Seek emergency care if hives are accompanied by throat swelling or difficulty breathing as this may indicate anaphylaxis.

Ringworm (Tinea):
Ringworm is a fungal infection causing a ring-shaped red scaly rash. It is highly contagious and spread by direct contact. Treatment involves antifungal creams, lotions, or oral antifungal medications for stubborn cases.

When to See a Dermatologist:
See a dermatologist for any skin condition that is worsening despite self-care, causes significant discomfort or affects quality of life, involves rapidly changing moles or spots, does not heal after 2 weeks, or if you notice any suspicious skin lesions that could indicate skin cancer.
